Engagement

The way I insisted we were engaged was not the right thing to do. It wasn’t something I was convinced I should, I however largely did it because I didn’t want to appear any less in my family’s eyes. This was misguided.

By posing the question “engaged or call it off” I made a terrible error. It may have felt that I was forcing you into it or perhaps giving you an ultimatum. In my defence, I would have backed off when i calmed down, stopped being triggered (explained later), and openly discussed why i wanted to do that with you so we can devise a way forward together.

There was also the cultural dimension we didn’t quite understand was at odds. As I previously explained, in my culture an engagement is when the couple decides they want to marry and the families get involved.

However, truthfully, I wasn’t thinking very clearly at that point. I felt this question should have been explored when both our families got together, and we had an open discussion on what it means to be engaged.

It was hurtful, (i feel) unlike me and I am truly sorry.

Playing

There is another thing I wanted to apologise for is for loosing control and playing (painfully) twice in the two and a half months your parents were here.

It’s no excuse, but the truth is I really needed some comfort in those moments from all the stress I was experiencing. However, all I achieved was upsetting you/making you feel weird.

I really did do my best to control that impulse, I think you saw that and wanted to help. But I shouldn’t have put you in that position.

I tried my best. Sometimes I fall short, and sometimes its not in my ability. I’m sorry if this is something that bothered you.
